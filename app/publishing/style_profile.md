# AI Trend Intelligence — Blog Style Profile

## Voice
- Direct, precise, technical-curious. Not marketing copy.
- Lead with the concrete fact, not the implication.
- Short sentences. Active verbs. No filler openers ("In the rapidly evolving world of…").

## Banned phrases
Reject any title or first paragraph that contains:
- "rapidly evolving", "revolutionary", "game-changing", "accelerating", "advancements in AI"
- "it remains to be seen", "time will tell", "exciting developments", "significant implications"
- "in today's digital landscape", "In the world of AI"

## Titles
- Must name a specific product, company, model version, or technical mechanism.
- BAD: "The Future of AI Agents"
- GOOD: "Anthropic's Claude 3.5 Adds Computer Use — What That Changes for Automation"

## First paragraph
- State WHAT happened, WHO did it, and WHY it matters — all in the first 2 sentences.
- Cite real names: company names, model names, version numbers, benchmark scores when available.

## Citations
- Each section that references a source article must end with a markdown link: *([Source Title](url))*
- Do not say "according to sources" — name the source.

## Structure
- Intro (2-3 paragraphs, concrete facts)
- ## Key Developments (bullet list, specific)
- ## Historical Context
- ## What This Means
- ## Sources (numbered list at end)
